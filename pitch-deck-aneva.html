The Science

Peer-reviewed research supporting voice-based credit assessment.

Loan Text ‚Üí Default Prediction

Netzer, Lemaire, Herzenstein (2019) ‚Äî "When Words Sweat." J. Marketing Research, 56(6). Loan application text predicts default beyond financial variables. Most directly relevant paper.

Voice ‚Üí Personality ‚Üí Behavior

Schuller et al. ‚Äî INTERSPEECH Computational Paralinguistics (2009‚Äìpresent). Acoustic features predict Big Five traits from speech.
Pentland, A. (2008) ‚Äî "Honest Signals" MIT Press. Non-linguistic vocal features predict social/economic outcomes.
McAleer et al. (2014) ‚Äî Personality + trustworthiness from a single "hello." PLoS ONE.

Voice ‚Üí Financial Outcomes

Mayew & Venkatachalam (2012) ‚Äî Vocal affect in earnings calls predicts firm performance. J. Finance, 67(1).
Hobson et al. (2012) ‚Äî Vocal dissonance predicts financial fraud. J. Accounting Research, 50(2).

Alternative Data ‚Üí Credit

Berg et al. (2020) ‚Äî Digital behavioral data predicts creditworthiness as well as FICO for thin-file. Rev. Financial Studies, 33(7).
Bjorkegren & Grissen (2020) ‚Äî Phone usage predicts loan default in developing countries. World Bank Econ. Review, 34(3).
Klinger & Khwaja (2013) ‚Äî Psychometric tests predict SME loan repayment in Peru/Colombia. Springer.

Convergence thesis: Voice ‚Üí personality (Schuller). Personality ‚Üí financial behavior (Klinger). Loan text ‚Üí default (Netzer). Voice in finance ‚Üí outcomes (Mayew, Hobson). Aneva sits at the intersection of all four.

Appendix D

US Regulatory & Fair Lending Analysis

FCRA, ECOA, and CFPB guidance on AI-powered credit decisions.

Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
Requires specific, accurate adverse action notices when denying credit. CFPB Circular 2023-03: AI complexity is not an excuse ‚Äî lenders must explain why in plain language, not broad buckets. Aneva's 8-dimension scoring with explicit justifications per dimension is inherently explainable ‚Äî each score comes with a written justification referencing applicant data.
‚ú¶ Compliant by design ‚Äî explainable scoring
Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA / Reg B)
Prohibits discrimination on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age. Voice data could theoretically encode proxies for protected classes (accent ‚Üí national origin, pitch ‚Üí gender). Mitigation: Aneva scores the content of what applicants say (repayment plan, employment) ‚Äî not vocal characteristics like pitch, accent, or speech patterns. The LLM analyzes the transcript text, not raw audio features.
‚ú¶ Content-based, not voice-feature-based
Disparate Impact Analysis
CFPB Jan 2025 Supervisory Highlights: all AI underwriting models must undergo disparate impact testing and search for Less Discriminatory Alternatives (LDAs). Note: The CFPB has proposed eliminating disparate impact liability under ECOA (Nov 2025 proposed rule), but this is not yet final. Aneva should conduct regular disparate impact audits regardless ‚Äî it's good practice and required for bank partnerships.
‚ö† Requires ongoing auditing ‚Äî landscape shifting
CFPB on AI & Black Box Models
CFPB has stated there is no technology exception to FCRA/ECOA. However, Aneva's architecture is uniquely positioned: the 8-dimension framework with explicit weights, per-dimension justifications, and clear decision rules (approve ‚â• 6.5, decline < 4.5) creates a transparent, auditable system ‚Äî more interpretable than typical ML-based credit models.
‚ú¶ More transparent than typical AI models
Voice as Alternative Data ‚Äî Risk Analysis
Key distinction: Aneva does not use voice biometrics, vocal emotion analysis, or acoustic feature extraction. The voice memo is merely the input modality ‚Äî Whisper converts it to text, then NLP analyzes the text content. This is functionally equivalent to a written application, just with a more accessible input method. This distinction is critical for regulators.
‚ú¶ Voice = input modality, not scoring signal
State-Level Enforcement
Even as federal enforcement softens, states are filling the gap. MA AG settled an AI lending case in July 2025 over disparate impact from institutional quality scoring. Aneva's "Education & Institutional Quality" dimension (0.15 weight) should be monitored for similar proxy effects. Recommend regular fair lending audits by state.
‚ö† Monitor state-level enforcement trends

Bottom line: Aneva's architecture is regulation-friendly by design. The transparent 8-dimension framework with explicit justifications exceeds the explainability bar set by CFPB. The critical insight: voice is the input modality, not a scoring feature ‚Äî the system analyzes what people say, not how they sound. Regular disparate impact audits and state-level monitoring are recommended as the regulatory landscape continues to evolve.
